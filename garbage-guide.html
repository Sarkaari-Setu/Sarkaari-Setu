Garbage Collection Complaint Guide

Learn how to report missed garbage collection, overflowing bins, unattended waste, and other waste management issues through your Municipal Corporation's official grievance portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Portal

Open your Municipal Corporation's official grievance or sanitation portal. Most local authorities provide online services for reporting garbage collection issues and tracking complaint status.

Tip: Report garbage collection issues as soon as possible to help maintain cleanliness and prevent health hazards.

📝 Step 2 — Register Your Complaint

Select the Garbage Collection Complaint service and carefully complete the complaint form.

  • Enter your contact details (if required).
  • Provide the exact location of the issue.
  • Select the complaint category.
  • Describe the problem clearly.
  • Review all details before submitting.

📷 Step 3 — Upload Supporting Evidence

Attach clear photographs and location details to help the sanitation department identify the issue quickly.

  • Photos of the garbage or overflowing bin.
  • Complete address or nearby landmark.
  • Additional comments (if required).
  • Other supporting documents (if applicable).
Clear photographs and an accurate location can help authorities resolve your complaint more efficiently.

🗑️ Step 4 — Common Garbage Collection Issues

You can report various waste collection problems, including:

  • Garbage not collected.
  • Overflowing public dustbins.
  • Missed door-to-door garbage collection.
  • Garbage dumped in public places.
  • Uncollected bulk waste.
  • Other waste management issues.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Complaint

After submitting your complaint, use the Complaint Number or Reference Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track complaint status.
  • View sanitation department updates.
  • Receive notifications from the authority.
  • Check for requests for additional information.
Keep your Complaint Number safe until your complaint has been resolved.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Provide the exact location of the garbage.
  • Upload clear photographs whenever possible.
  • Avoid submitting duplicate complaints.
  • Track your complaint regularly.
  • Keep your Complaint Number for future reference.
  • Use only the official Municipal Corporation portal.
Submit only genuine complaints with accurate information. False or misleading complaints may delay public services and could result in action under applicable local regulations.
