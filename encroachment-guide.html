Encroachment Complaint Guide

Learn how to report unauthorized encroachments on public land, roads, footpaths, parks, or government property, upload supporting evidence, track your complaint, and receive updates through your Municipal Corporation or local government portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Portal

Open your Municipal Corporation's official grievance or citizen services portal. Most local authorities allow citizens to report encroachments online and track the status of their complaints.

Tip: File a complaint only for genuine cases of unauthorized occupation or construction on public land or government property.

📝 Step 2 — Register Your Complaint

Select the Encroachment Complaint service and carefully fill out the complaint form.

  • Enter your contact details (if required).
  • Provide the exact location of the encroachment.
  • Select the type of encroachment.
  • Describe the issue clearly.
  • Review the complaint before submitting.

📷 Step 3 — Upload Supporting Evidence

Attach clear evidence to help the concerned authority verify your complaint.

  • Photographs of the encroachment.
  • Location details or address.
  • Supporting documents (if available).
  • Any additional information that may help the investigation.
Clear photographs and accurate location details can help authorities process your complaint more efficiently.

📋 Step 4 — Submit & Track Your Complaint

After submitting the complaint, note your Complaint Number or Reference Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track complaint status.
  • View inspection updates.
  • Receive notifications from the authority.
  • Check if additional information is requested.

🏛️ Step 5 — Verification & Action

The concerned municipal authority may inspect the reported location and take action according to applicable laws and regulations.

  • Site inspection.
  • Verification of complaint.
  • Action by the competent authority, if applicable.
  • Status updates on the portal.
The time taken to resolve complaints may vary depending on the nature of the case and local authority procedures.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Provide accurate location details.
  • Upload clear photographs whenever possible.
  • Avoid filing duplicate complaints for the same issue.
  • Keep your Complaint Number safe.
  • Track your complaint regularly.
  • Use only the official Municipal Corporation portal.
Submit complaints truthfully and avoid providing false or misleading information. False complaints may result in action under applicable laws or local regulations.
