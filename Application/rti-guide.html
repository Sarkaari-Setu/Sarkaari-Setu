RTI Application Guide

Learn how to file an RTI (Right to Information) Application, submit the required details, pay the application fee, track your request, and obtain information from public authorities through the official central RTI portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official RTI Portal

Open the official Central Government RTI Online Portal (rtionline.gov.in). This portal allows Indian citizens to electronically submit RTI applications and first appeals to all Central Ministries, Departments, and Public Authorities.

Tip: This portal is strictly for Central Government authorities. For State Government departments (e.g., state police, local municipal bodies), use your respective state’s dedicated RTI portal.

📝 Step 2 — Prepare Your RTI Request

Before filling out the online form, prepare your query clearly to avoid delays or rejections:

  • Write specific questions with exact dates, file numbers, or department names if available.
  • Request existing records, documents, orders, or reports—avoid asking for opinions, hypothetical scenarios, or justifications.
  • Keep the main text within the portal’s 3,000-character limit. (If longer, attach a PDF document without spaces in its filename).
  • Do not attach identity documents like Aadhaar or PAN unless attaching a Below Poverty Line (BPL) certificate for fee exemption.
Well-structured RTI applications are easier for Central Public Information Officers (CPIOs) to process and respond to promptly.

📋 Step 3 — Submit the RTI Application

Click on the Submit Request option on the main page, accept the portal guidelines, and complete the form:

  • Select the correct Public Authority (Ministry or Department) from the dropdown list.
  • Enter applicant details (Name, Address, Phone Number, and Email ID for receiving SMS/email updates).
  • Type your specific RTI query in the prescribed text area.
  • Review all filled information carefully before proceeding to payment.

💳 Step 4 — Pay the Application Fee

Complete the ₹10 application fee online using any of the integrated payment modes:

  • Select from Net Banking, Credit/Debit Cards (MasterCard, Visa, RuPay), or UPI.
  • If you hold a BPL card, select the BPL option and upload your valid government-issued BPL certificate to waive the fee.
  • Save the payment receipt and note down the transaction reference number.
If additional fees are required later for photocopying or printing large volumes of documents (e.g., ₹2 per page), the CPIO will notify you through the portal.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Application

After successful payment, a unique Registration Number will be generated. Use it to monitor your application:

  • Click on the View Status tab on the home page.
  • Enter your Registration Number, Email ID, and the OTP sent to your email.
  • Track progress, see whether it has been transferred to another CPIO, or check for additional fee requests.

📥 Step 6 — Receive the Information

By law, the concerned public authority must provide a response within 30 days of receipt.

  • Download available PDF documents directly from the portal’s View Status section.
  • Review the provided information carefully.
  • If information is refused, incomplete, or not provided within 30 days, file a First Appeal online free of charge through the same portal using your original Registration Number.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Keep your questions precise and stick strictly to existing, documented facts.
  • Provide a mobile number during submission to receive instant SMS updates.
  • If payment is debited but a Registration Number isn't generated immediately, wait 24–48 hours for system reconciliation before attempting again.
  • Save digital or physical copies of your registration details for any future appeals.
Do not include sensitive personal identification (like Aadhaar card or PAN card details) in your text or attachments. Always ensure you are on the official central URL (rtionline.gov.in) to protect your security and avoid fake third-party portals.
