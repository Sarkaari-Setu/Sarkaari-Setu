Sanitation Complaint Guide

Learn how to report sanitation-related issues, upload supporting evidence, track your complaint, and receive updates through your Municipal Corporation or local government portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Portal

Open your Municipal Corporation's official grievance or citizen services portal. Most local authorities provide online services for reporting sanitation issues and tracking complaint status.

Tip: Report sanitation problems as soon as you notice them to help maintain public hygiene and safety.

📝 Step 2 — Register Your Complaint

Select the Sanitation Complaint service and carefully complete the complaint form.

  • Enter your contact details (if required).
  • Provide the exact location of the issue.
  • Select the complaint category.
  • Describe the sanitation problem clearly.
  • Review all details before submission.

📷 Step 3 — Upload Supporting Evidence

Attach clear photographs or supporting information to help the concerned department understand the issue.

  • Photos of the sanitation problem.
  • Location details or address.
  • Additional comments (if necessary).
  • Other supporting documents (if applicable).
Providing clear photographs and an accurate location helps the authorities resolve your complaint more efficiently.

🧹 Step 4 — Types of Sanitation Complaints

Municipal Corporations commonly accept complaints related to:

  • Garbage not collected.
  • Overflowing community dustbins.
  • Dirty streets or public places.
  • Improper waste disposal.
  • Open dumping of garbage.
  • Other sanitation-related issues.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Complaint

After submitting your complaint, use the Complaint Number or Reference Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track complaint status.
  • View inspection updates.
  • Receive notifications from the authority.
  • Check for any additional information requests.
Keep your Complaint Number safe until the issue has been resolved.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Provide the correct location of the issue.
  • Upload clear photographs whenever possible.
  • Avoid submitting duplicate complaints.
  • Track your complaint regularly.
  • Keep your Complaint Number for future reference.
  • Use only the official Municipal Corporation portal.
Submit only genuine complaints and provide accurate information. False or misleading complaints may delay services and could lead to action under applicable local regulations.
