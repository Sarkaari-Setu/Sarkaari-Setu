Grievance / Complaint Application Guide

Learn how to submit a grievance or complaint, upload supporting documents, track your application, and receive updates through your government department's or public authority's official grievance portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Grievance Portal

Open the official grievance portal of the concerned Government Department, Public Authority, Municipal Corporation, or the Centralized Public Grievance Redress and Monitoring System (CPGRAMS), if applicable.

Tip: Submit your grievance to the appropriate department to ensure faster processing and resolution.

📝 Step 2 — Register Your Grievance

Select the Grievance / Complaint service and carefully complete the online application form.

  • Enter your personal details.
  • Select the concerned department or authority.
  • Choose the grievance category.
  • Describe the issue clearly and accurately.
  • Review all information before submission.

📄 Step 3 — Upload Supporting Documents

Attach relevant documents or evidence to support your grievance.

  • Identity Proof (if required).
  • Supporting documents.
  • Photographs or screenshots (if applicable).
  • Previous correspondence or reference numbers.
  • Any other relevant evidence.
Providing complete and relevant supporting documents helps the concerned authority understand and resolve your grievance more efficiently.

📨 Step 4 — Submit Your Application

Review all details carefully before submitting your grievance.

  • Verify all entered information.
  • Submit the grievance.
  • Save the acknowledgement or registration number.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Grievance

Use your Registration Number, Complaint Number, or Reference Number to monitor the progress of your grievance.

  • Track application status.
  • View departmental responses.
  • Receive status notifications.
  • Provide additional information if requested.
Keep your Registration Number safe until your grievance has been resolved.

✅ Step 6 — Review the Resolution

Once your grievance has been processed, review the response provided by the concerned authority.

  • Read the official response carefully.
  • Download available documents or reports.
  • Save the final communication for future reference.
  • Use the available appeal or feedback process if applicable.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Submit your grievance to the correct department.
  • Provide accurate and complete information.
  • Upload clear supporting documents.
  • Keep your registration number safe.
  • Track your grievance regularly.
  • Use only official government grievance portals.
Submit only genuine grievances with truthful information. Providing false or misleading information may delay the resolution process and could result in action under applicable laws or departmental rules.
